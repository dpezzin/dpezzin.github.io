Hi! I’m Dan Pezzin. I love business and technology. I decided computers were really cool during my senior year at SJJ ‘07 (link) when a buddy from yearbook class showed me how to Photoshop his face onto an NBA poster. The timing was perfect! Everyone was getting their college acceptance letters and .edu email addresses, so we all signed up for Facebook at the exact same time. I became an expert at downloading profile pictures and uploading my own funny Photoshopped versions of friends with celebrities. It helped me learn basic computer skills and the fundamentals of the windows directory structure without having to learn how to read code.

I decided not to pursue computer science in college, because code looked like gibberish to me and all the symbols were intimidating. Instead, I got my undergrad degree in business from the University of Toledo, majoring in entrepreneurship and marketing. On the side, I spent free time teaching myself how to build apps on my original Motorola Droid with Google’s App Inventor.

App Inventor was the second best discovery I have ever made. The Droid was a popular phone at the time. My friends all had them too, so my apps ran on all of our phones. There was no code to learn with App Inventor. The controls were all drag and drop, so it was really easy to pick up. App Inventor projects still had the capability to use advanced features like GPS and messaging. You could also download open source App Inventor projects that other people have built and make changes to them.

Even though I have upgraded to building more complex apps with Java now, my favorite Android app I have ever built was my second App Inventor project. It let me hack my friend’s text messages and auto reply with my message instead. For two hours, his phone was telling everyone “I need to call you back I’m busy putting on lipstick and dresses.” I thought it was awesome that I could use technology to pull pranks on my friends in college and I wanted to learn more, but I wasn’t sure how.

Earlier I said that App Inventor was my second best discovery. The very best discovery I have ever made was the free online computer science program offered by the Harvard Extension School. All of the lectures are recorded on Harvard’s campus and uploaded to the course websites. They go pretty fast. I had to pause the videos a lot to take notes, but the material is covered in perfect order. I took CS1, CS50, CS75, CS76. I recommend CS1 as a perfect introduction to anyone looking to improve their overall computer skills. CS50 and CS75 are introductory programming classes and CS76 covers mobile development. I was comfortable with most of the front-end technologies after taking these courses, but less comfortable with the backend.

Then I came across a program funded by the Department of Labor. It was called .NET Code Reconnect. They are trying to turn Detroit, Michigan into the technology capital of the world by offering free Microsoft courses 480, 483, and 486, but I have heard there are similar programs across throughout the United States. The material covered the .NET framework version 4.5, C#, HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and building applications with MVC4. The program was hard to get into. They only accepted candidates with a college degree who could pass two interviews, a background check, and a 30 question entrance exam, but I would recommend these programs to anyone who has time to apply. I learned a ton and it’s actually the reason I’m building this website right now and many more projects to come. Stay tuned.
